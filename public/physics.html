Magnetic Force

I started this because I was sure that today's magnetic force equation that uses the right hand law is wrong. Lets take a look at the thing that triggered me the most:
Both particles are positivly charged. The green particle goes right, and the yellow particle goes up. As you can see the yellow particle pushes green down, and the green particle doesnt creates any force at all on yellow.



So I was trying to find new equation that will make a lot more sense. If you wanna see how i found a new equation, check [my equation discovery](discovering_equation.html).

New Equation
[Here](comparing_equations.html) you can check why the new equation and the right hand law is almost the same thing. Shortly the new equation is: $$F=\frac{(\vec I_1\ \bot\ \vec r)\cdot(\vec I_2\ \bot\ \vec r)}{r^2}$$
The force direction is always in \\(\hat r\\) direction. Now all I had to do is to compare the equations, and find the biggest difference between them to make an experiment and prove that the right hand law is wrong.
